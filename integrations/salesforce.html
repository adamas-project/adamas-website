The decision-memory layer for Salesforce

Salesforce is the system of record for your revenue — but it records what, not why. The judgement behind opportunity stages, forecasts and won/lost calls lives in Chatter, notes and field history, scattered and unstructured.

ADAMAS is the decision ledger that connects to Salesforce and captures the why behind every decision made there — structuring it into a permanent, searchable record your whole company can use. It doesn't replace Salesforce; it gives it a memory.

Where decisions disappear in Salesforce

Opportunity stage history shows the path a deal took. It doesn't preserve the reasoning behind each move, the forecast call, or the decision to walk away.

For a growing company, that lost reasoning is exactly what diligence, board reviews and new-hire ramp depend on — and Salesforce alone doesn't keep it in a usable form.

How ADAMAS connects to Salesforce

ADAMAS plugs into Salesforce and continuously reads the places where decisions and their reasoning live:

  • Opportunity and stage changes, with the owner of each call
  • Chatter and notes — where the reasoning is discussed
  • Won / lost and forecast decisions, structured and linked
  • Account context preserved alongside every decision
Example

An opportunity is downgraded in the forecast. ADAMAS captures the decision, reads the Chatter thread for the rationale (“legal review slipping to next quarter”), records who called it, and links it to similar forecast moves — building a decision trail diligence will love.

Salesforce + ADAMAS — FAQ

Is ADAMAS a Salesforce replacement?

No. ADAMAS is a decision-memory layer on top of Salesforce. Salesforce stays your CRM; ADAMAS captures and structures the reasoning behind the decisions made inside it.

How does ADAMAS integrate with Salesforce?

Through the Salesforce API and event streams — ADAMAS reads opportunity and stage changes, Chatter and notes, then records a structured decision entry.

Does it move data to a third-party cloud?

No. ADAMAS is local-first and runs on your own hardware. External routing happens only for tasks you explicitly approve.

Does it work with the rest of our stack?

Yes — ADAMAS unifies reasoning from Salesforce, comms, meetings and documents into a single decision ledger.
