The decision-memory layer for Slack

A huge share of your company's real decisions happen in Slack — in a thread, between two people, in the flow of a busy day. Then they scroll away. Slack search finds messages; it doesn't preserve decisions.

ADAMAS is the decision ledger that connects to Slack and captures the why behind every decision made there — structuring it into a permanent, searchable record your whole company can use. It doesn't replace Slack; it gives it a memory.

Where decisions disappear in Slack

The reasoning behind a Slack decision is right there in the thread — for about a week. After that it's effectively gone, buried under thousands of newer messages and impossible to reconstruct.

The faster your team moves in Slack, the more institutional reasoning you quietly lose. The very speed that makes Slack great is what makes its decisions evaporate.

How ADAMAS connects to Slack

ADAMAS plugs into Slack and continuously reads the places where decisions and their reasoning live:

  • Channels and threads where decisions are debated and made
  • The reasoning and trade-offs discussed in the conversation
  • Who decided and when, captured before it scrolls away
  • Links to related decisions across channels
Example

A thread in #product debates two architectures and lands on one. ADAMAS recognises the decision, captures the choice and the reasoning (“go local-first, revisit at 25 staff”), records who decided, and writes it to the ledger — so the call survives long after the thread is buried.

Slack + ADAMAS — FAQ

Does ADAMAS read all our Slack messages?

ADAMAS focuses on identifying and structuring decisions and their reasoning from the channels you connect — not on archiving every message. You control which channels are in scope.

How does the Slack integration work?

Through Slack's API and events — ADAMAS reads the threads you connect, recognises decisions and the reasoning around them, and writes structured entries to your ledger.

Where is the data kept?

Local-first, on hardware you own. Nothing is sent to a third-party cloud unless you explicitly approve it.

Can it combine Slack with our CRM and docs?

Yes — ADAMAS unifies reasoning from Slack, your CRM, meetings and documents into one decision ledger.
