The decision-memory layer for HubSpot

HubSpot is an excellent CRM for tracking what happened with a deal. But the reasoning that actually drives revenue — why you qualified a lead, why you discounted, why a deal was won or lost — gets buried in notes, emails and logged calls.

ADAMAS is the decision ledger that connects to HubSpot and captures the why behind every decision made there — structuring it into a permanent, searchable record your whole company can use. It doesn't replace HubSpot; it gives it a memory.

Where decisions disappear in HubSpot

Deal stages in HubSpot tell you where a deal is. They don't tell you the judgement behind each move. When a rep leaves, their pipeline reasoning leaves with them, and the next person inherits records without rationale.

Quarter after quarter, the most valuable sales asset you have — the pattern of why you win and lose — stays trapped in scattered notes instead of compounding into institutional knowledge.

How ADAMAS connects to HubSpot

ADAMAS plugs into HubSpot and continuously reads the places where decisions and their reasoning live:

  • Deals and deal-stage changes, with the owner who made the call
  • Notes, logged calls and emails — where qualification and pricing reasoning lives
  • Won / lost reasons, structured and linked across deals
  • Company and contact context, so decisions keep their situation
Example

A deal is marked Closed Lost. ADAMAS captures the decision, reads the associated notes and emails for the real reason (“chose an in-house build”), records who decided, and links it to similar losses — turning your HubSpot history into a win/loss intelligence layer.

HubSpot + ADAMAS — FAQ

Is ADAMAS a HubSpot alternative?

No. ADAMAS complements HubSpot. You keep HubSpot as your CRM; ADAMAS captures the reasoning behind the decisions made inside it and builds a permanent decision ledger.

How does the HubSpot integration work?

ADAMAS connects through HubSpot's API and webhooks to read deals, deal-stage changes, notes and logged activities, then writes a structured decision entry for each meaningful call.

Does our data leave HubSpot for a third-party cloud?

No. ADAMAS is local-first and runs on hardware you own. Nothing is sent to third-party clouds unless you explicitly approve it for a specific task.

What if we also use a project tool?

ADAMAS unifies reasoning across your whole stack — HubSpot for sales plus your PM tool, comms and docs — into a single decision ledger.
