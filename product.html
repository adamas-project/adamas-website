Inside the vault.

Five views of the system you get: how decisions are captured, stored, linked, and turned into assets — and the one dialog that guards your data boundary.

Prefer to click instead of read? Open the interactive ledger demo →

High-fidelity mockups — real screenshots in preparation
adamas — vault · ledger
01

The ledger

The core of the vault: every decision is a structured entry — context, the exact call, who made it, the trade-offs accepted, and two-way links to the decisions it shaped.

Filter by domain, follow a link, and the reasoning behind any call from two years ago is three clicks away. Try it in the demo →

adamas — capture inbox
02

The capture inbox

Hermes reads the week's noise — Slack threads, email, transcripts, tickets — and surfaces the moments that were actually decisions. You confirm or dismiss; nothing enters the ledger unreviewed.

This is the "runs on autopilot" part: capture costs the team minutes per week, not a documentation habit nobody keeps.

adamas — decision graph
03

The decision graph

Decisions connect to each other. Declining one big contract touches pricing, billing, and staffing — and the graph shows those links. When someone asks "why are we set up this way?", the answer is on screen instead of reconstructed from memory.

This is also what compounds: every new entry adds edges, and the graph becomes your company's institutional memory.

adamas — asset generation
04

Asset generation

Pick the asset your stage needs — onboarding doc, hiring framework, investor material — and ADAMAS assembles it from the ledger, with every section traced to its source decisions.

When a decision changes, the asset regenerates. The documentation stays current instead of going out of date. Read a full generated sample →

adamas — hybrid approval
05

The boundary dialog

The only moment data can leave your machine — and it never happens silently. You see exactly what would be transmitted, approve or decline per task, and the route is logged in the ledger.

Decline, and the task runs locally instead. The default is local-first: data leaves only for hybrid-cloud tasks you explicitly approve here, and for integrations you connect. Full security details →

ADAMAS (All Decisions And Memory Archive System) is a product of Falcon Intelligence Group LLC, built on the Hermes AI agent. Hermes is a third-party tool and the property of its respective owner.

Local-first by design: your vault and decision ledger run on hardware you own. ADAMAS does not send your company's knowledge to third-party clouds — optional hybrid cloud routing is used only for tasks you explicitly approve.

Pricing shown is indicative and may vary with scope. The Clarity Audit fee is credited toward your build if you proceed. Client hardware is purchased and owned by the client. The named devices are examples, not requirements — ADAMAS is hardware-flexible; as a 2026 reference, an Apple Mac mini M4 runs roughly $600–$1,000 and 128 GB AMD Ryzen AI Max+ or NVIDIA DGX Spark machines roughly $1,800–$4,000. A typical local-first setup replaces about $200–459/month in cloud-AI subscriptions.

Interface views on this page are high-fidelity mockups with sample data; they illustrate the system's design and may differ from the shipped product.
