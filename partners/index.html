Brody runs several businesses at once — the case ADAMAS is built for, where the reasoning behind a decision has to outlast the day it was made. He backed ADAMAS as its first investor and runs it as its first user: every venture in his portfolio records its decisions in the ledger. We don't ask anyone to trust ADAMAS with anything its own investor doesn't use it for first.

ADAMAS (All Decisions And Memory Archive System) is a product of Falcon Intelligence Group LLC, built on the Hermes AI agent. Hermes is a third-party tool and the property of its respective owner.

Local-first by design: your vault and decision ledger run on hardware you own. ADAMAS does not send your company's knowledge to third-party clouds — optional hybrid cloud routing is used only for tasks you explicitly approve.

Pricing shown is indicative and may vary with scope. The Clarity Audit fee is credited toward your build if you proceed. Client hardware is purchased and owned by the client. The named devices are examples, not requirements — ADAMAS is hardware-flexible; as a 2026 reference, an Apple Mac mini M4 runs roughly $600–$1,000 and 128 GB AMD Ryzen AI Max+ or NVIDIA DGX Spark machines roughly $1,800–$4,000. A typical local-first setup replaces about $200–459/month in cloud-AI subscriptions.
