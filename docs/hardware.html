Recommended hardware

ADAMAS runs entirely on a machine you own. It is hardware-flexible: anything that can run the local Hermes model and stay powered on 24/7 will work. Below are three configurations we have tested, from a quiet default to a large-model option. These are examples, not requirements.

Apple Mac mini (M4), 24–32 GB — recommended for most teams

  • Quietest and most power-efficient option; near-silent, a few dollars a month in electricity.
  • Runs Hermes comfortably for capture, evaluation, and asset generation at the model sizes ADAMAS uses.
  • Simplest to set up and maintain — no driver management.
  • Approx. 2026 price: ~$600–$1,000, depending on memory and storage.

AMD Ryzen AI Max+ 395 mini PC (e.g. GMKtec EVO-X2), 128 GB — more local headroom

  • 128 GB of unified memory lets Hermes run larger 70B-class models entirely on-device for higher-fidelity assets.
  • x86/Linux; integrates with the same local pipeline.
  • Slightly more initial configuration (handled during your build); fan audible but not intrusive under load.
  • Approx. 2026 price: ~$1,800–$2,000.

NVIDIA DGX Spark (GB10), 128 GB — optional, for CUDA/NVIDIA environments

  • 128 GB unified memory with the full CUDA stack; strong for prototyping and on-device fine-tuning.
  • Premium price; for ADAMAS's core capture-and-generate workload it is more than most teams need, and large-model token speed is bandwidth-limited.
  • Approx. 2026 price: ~$3,000–$4,000.

All three run ADAMAS fully offline, expose no inbound ports, and keep the ledger, source material, embeddings, and generated assets on the machine. Optional hybrid-cloud tasks remain opt-in and are logged. A typical local-first setup replaces roughly $200–459/month in cloud-AI subscriptions (run your own numbers).
