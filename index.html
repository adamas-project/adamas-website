The Problem

The most valuable asset in your company is trapped in a few heads.

It lives in Slack threads, whiteboards, call recordings, and the founder's memory. Today's tools store information — none capture the reasoning behind the decision.

When people leave

Critical context walks out the door. Onboarding the next person takes months because nothing was ever written down in a structured way.

When investors ask "why?"

"Why did you choose X over Y?" There's no record of the decision, the context, or who made the call. The reasoning is gone.

When the founder is the bottleneck

You're too small for a COO, too large to do it all yourself. You can't delegate because the knowledge has never been extracted from your head.

When decisions get repeated

In engineering and technical work, a single missed decision context can cost $50k–$500k in rework. Decision amnesia is expensive.

"Not what you should do next — but what you and your company decided, and why."

You can't predict the future. You're the first to master the past — every decision your company makes, and the why behind it, captured and never lost again.

Honest Answers

The questions founders actually ask.

We already use Notion / Obsidian / spreadsheets.

Those tools store information. ADAMAS captures the reasoning behind the decision and generates the next asset automatically. The difference is between a filing cabinet and an operating system.

We can just write things down.

You already know that. The problem isn't writing — it's structuring, linking, and surfacing the right knowledge at the right moment. ADAMAS does that automatically.

Is this just AI hype?

The AI is the engine, not the product. The product is the decision ledger — a structured, local-first knowledge layer that compounds over time regardless of which model powers it.
